Why use Murmur

User owned data

This means that any information uploaded by any individual, is completely owned by them, and not by any platform or the underlying software/network. This gives users control over their data, and they can choose to share it with ad networks that can build on top of Murmur. This is radically different from centralized networks, where all user data is generated, analysed, and monetized by a central party.

Prevent fake news

Every transaction on a blockchain is traceable to its origin. This helps minimize fake news by verifying the source behind false information. Such bad actors who spread fake news can then be made publicly known to prevent further spreading and also can be blocked from EOS through the EOS constitution. This is unlike a centralized network where a domino effect can cause irrevocable social damage.

Interact with peers openly or discretely

Similar to centralized content publishing platforms, you can Snoop, comment, and yell a ‘murmur’. You can interact one to one through a fully secret and encrypted chat by Whispering to a peer. What you publish is logged permanently on the blockchain and your private messages are fully encrypted.

Build innovative applications on Murmur

Applications such as advertisement and payment networks can build on top of Murmur with our open-source SDKs and APIs. The overlying value of these networks then goes directly to the users, who can get rewarded for hosting relevant ads on their profile or transact freely and securely with their peers.

Features

EOS Account creation and delegation

Create new EOS public/private key pairs stored locally on your phone. Create EOS Accounts, delegate your account to friends in case of contingencies.

Incentive model

Users take back value for being part of murmur, in the form of the MUR token -- paid for by the network and partner platforms

Protocol integration

murmur supports partner platforms with APIs and SDKs -- enabling possibilities such as ad networks which pay the user and the signing into third party apps with murmur.

Decentralization for privacy

Cryptographic encryption of private data along with a decentralized validation network equals to consent driven user privacy.

Centralization for usability

murmur bridges the gap in the practicality of public blockchains with partial centralization -- only by keeping a remote copy os public user activity.

Data Traceability

It's possible to trace the origins of any story or message as all interactions with the App happens through the EOS Blockchain. This helps determine origins of fake news that is proliferating across social media.

User Actions

Murmur

This action allows the user to publicly broadcast a short message of 140 characters to all their followers. This is the primary way of information input into the network. Every murmur is broadcasted on the EOS Blockchain.

Yell

A murmur can then be yelled by anyone who sees it – this action shares the original mumur with all the followers of the person yelling the murmur. A yell by a user is recorded on EOS.

Whisper

A Whisper is a private encrypted message sent to another user. The contents of the message stream are end-to-end encrypted, and no one other than the two users can ever gain access to it.

Snoop

This is a way for users to show anonymous appreciation for any of the murmurs visible to them. Other users can always see the number of snoops a murmur has gathered, but they can never know who’s really behind it. In a decentralized context, this helps users back information they feel strongly about, without necessarily having to be known for doing so.

Storing on the Blockchain

User actions from the front-end, trigger functions embedded into the smart contract.
The contract then writes activity data onto subsequent blocks being produced.
Our remote server keeps a copy of this public data, to provide users a more seamless and fast experience.

Retrieving from the Blockchain

User activity published on the blockchain is continuously fed into our remote server.
This data is used to feed the front-end, and show users what all their friends are up to!
